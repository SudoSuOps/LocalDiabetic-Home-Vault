# Daily Foot Check

**Foot care matters more for diabetics than almost anything else at home.**
A small sore caught early stays small. Check both feet **every day**.

> If you find any of the warning signs below, **call your doctor or podiatrist.**
> This checklist organizes a routine — it does not diagnose.

---

## The 2-minute daily check (both feet)

Look at the top, bottom, sides, and between every toe. Use a mirror or ask for help
if you can't see the bottoms.

- ☐ Any **cuts, cracks, or sores**?
- ☐ Any **redness or warm spots**?
- ☐ Any **swelling**?
- ☐ Any **blisters or calluses**?
- ☐ Any **color change** (pale, blue, dark)?
- ☐ Any **drainage or odor**?
- ☐ **Numbness, tingling, or pain**?
- ☐ Toenails okay (not ingrown, not too long)?

## Daily care

- ☐ Washed feet with warm (not hot) water
- ☐ Dried gently, **especially between the toes**
- ☐ Moisturized tops and bottoms (**not** between the toes)
- ☐ Wore clean, dry socks
- ☐ Checked inside shoes for pebbles or rough spots before putting them on

## 🚩 Call the doctor today if you see:

- A sore that isn't healing, or is getting bigger
- Redness spreading, increasing warmth, or red streaks
- Pus, drainage, or a bad smell
- A black or dark area
- Fever along with any foot problem
